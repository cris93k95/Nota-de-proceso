PART I — Installation Report (10 points)

Questions 1–5. Read the report and choose the best answer. Each correct answer is worth 2 points.

Text 1 — LED Lighting Project
In the school workshop, an electricity group planned a new LED lighting circuit for a study room. The first survey showed that the old fluorescent lamps used too much energy and produced uneven light. The students read the wiring diagram, calculated the load, and selected two separate circuits: one for the main lights and another for emergency lighting. During the installation, they labelled each conductor and checked the voltage with a multimeter before connecting the switches. When the project was finished, the room was brighter and the panel was easier to understand during maintenance.

A) To repair the school internet network.
B) To install a new LED lighting circuit in a study room.
C) To replace the laboratory computers.
D) To move the electrical panel to another building.

2. What problem did the old fluorescent lamps cause?

A) They used too much energy and produced uneven light.
B) They disconnected the emergency exit signs.
C) They made the multimeter stop working.
D) They blocked the wiring diagram completely.

3. Why did the group select two separate circuits?

A) To connect one circuit to the internet.
B) To separate main lighting from emergency lighting.
C) To avoid using any labels on conductors.
D) To increase the classroom temperature.

4. Which tool was used to check voltage before connecting the switches?

A) A welding helmet.
B) A multimeter.
C) A paint roller.
D) A brake scanner.

5. What positive result is mentioned at the end of the text?

A) The room became brighter and the panel was easier to understand.
B) The group removed the emergency lights completely.
C) The old lamps produced more heat than before.
D) The switches were connected without measuring voltage.
PART II — Isolation Procedure (10 points)

Questions 6–10. Read the procedure and identify the correct sequence for safe work.

Text 2 — Breaker Replacement Procedure
Before replacing a miniature breaker, the technician reviews the panel diagram and confirms which circuit must be isolated. Then the main supply is switched off, a lockout device is placed on the handle, and a warning tag is added. After that, the technician uses a tester to confirm that no voltage is present. Only then is the damaged breaker removed and the new unit installed. At the end, the connections are tightened, the panel cover is replaced, and the circuit is tested again under supervision.

6. What is checked before the technician touches the breaker?

A) The panel diagram and the circuit to isolate.
B) The school timetable.
C) The paint colour of the panel.
D) The classroom attendance list.

7. What is placed on the handle after the main supply is switched off?

A) A cleaning cloth.
B) A lockout device.
C) A new conductor label.
D) A battery charger.

8. Why is a tester used before the damaged breaker is removed?

A) To confirm that no voltage is present.
B) To measure room temperature.
C) To print a maintenance invoice.
D) To connect the emergency circuit.

9. What happens after the new breaker is installed?

A) The wiring diagram is deleted.
B) The connections are tightened and the cover is replaced.
C) The voltage is left untested.
D) The old breaker is reinstalled.

10. How is the final circuit test performed?

A) Without supervision.
B) Only after removing the cover forever.
C) Again under supervision.

PART III — Safety Notice (10 points)

Questions 11–15. Read the notice and identify the key precautions and the main risk being prevented in the work area.

Text 3 — Temporary Wiring Safety Notice
SAFETY NOTICE: Keep extension cords away from wet floors and doorways. Replace any conductor that shows broken insulation. Do not overload a multi-plug adapter with several high-consumption devices. Before using a portable drill, inspect the plug and confirm that the ground connection is intact. Report any unusual smell or excessive heat to the teacher immediately.

A) Wet floors and doorways.
B) Clean notebooks only.
C) The lighting diagram only.
D) The teacher's desk only.

12. What should be replaced immediately?

A) Any conductor with broken insulation.
B) Every new LED lamp.
C) The panel cover after each class.
D) Every lockout tag.

C) The study room window.

14. What must be confirmed before using a portable drill?

C) The colour of the extension cord.
D) The age of the classroom panel.

15. What overall problem does this notice mainly help prevent?
